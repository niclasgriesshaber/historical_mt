
### Example Translations:

Example 1
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Der Schulteß Zu Mildenaw, hatt Hanß Nichten vnd Mertin Lucaßen Zu Purgen gesatzt, das er sich von dato Zwischen hier vnd Sonnabents richtig Zahlen vnd befridigen will, Sig: Fridland den 23 Octobris A.o 85.

Translation to English:
The village headman of Mildenau names Hans Nicht and Mertin Lucass as pledges that he will pay correctly between now and Saturday, Friedland 23 Oct 1585


Example 2
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Jacob Seidel von hainerßdorff, demnach er Michael Schwerdnern mit ehrrurigen wortten angriffen darumb er gefenglich eingezogen vnd zu Purgen wiederumb außgeben, Also vnd dergestaldt, das er gemeltten Michael schwerdtnern hinfurter genzlichen zu frieden vnnd vngeschmehet sol laßen, auch wan er gefodert wirdt sich wieder gestelle, dafur Purge Christoff drumel von hainerßdorff Signatum den 27 Martij Ao p 88.

Translation to English:
Jacob Seidel from Hainersdorf, since he attacked Michael Schwerdner with defamatory words, for which reason he was taken into imprisonment and released again on pledges, thus and as follows, that he shall leave the said Michael Schwerdtner henceforth completely in peace and undefamed, also report back when he is summoned, for which is pledge Christoff Drumel from Hainersdorf, signed 27 Mar 1588


Example 3
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Auf Grundtmans Klage antwortett der Scholtz, das er gestehe, das der grundtman schelmisch hinter seinen Rücken im Ambt geklagt, hierauff Grundtman die Vnwarheit dargethan, Vndt denselben Zalung der 12 fessell Bier 8 Arg. Vndt 13 g. auferlegt worden. Ist das geldt dem scholzen Zugesteltt, Abbitt erfolgtt, Vndt der Scholz hierumb weg. seines Vngehorsambs auf den Ambt befehl gebührlich gestrafft worden. Ferners Klagt der Scholz Vber Görge wagnern Zu gestehen, wegen schuldt, Ist vngehorsamblich außblieben, Vndt als er gleich mit eim Eltesten nach ihm geschickt, sey er nicht kommen. Reus R.s.ed.et: habe bitten lassen, er wolle sich mit ihm gedulden, dan er scheren muessen, der Elteste sagt auß, das er ihn ermahnet Zuegestehen, hette er gesagt, Er wolle wol komen, es würde sich wol schicken. Er seye dem Scholtzen 3 Görlizsche M. schuldig, solls Verbürgen, den nehistem tag. zu Zahlen.

Translation to English:
To Grundtman's complaint the village headman answers that he admits that Grundtman had villainously complained behind his back in the manorial court, hereupon Grundtman had demonstrated untruth, and the latter had been instructed to payment of the 12 barrels of beer 8 Argents and 13 Groschen,s the money was delivered to the village headman, apology occurred, and concerning this the village headman was properly punished concerning his disobedience to the manorial court's command. Further the village headman complains against Goerge Wagner to admit, concerning debt, he disobediently remained away, and when he immediately sent after him with an elder, he did not come. Reus R.S.Ed.Et: he had sent the request that he would have patience with him, for he had to do the shearing; the village elder gives testimony, that he warned him to report, he had said, he would indeed come, it would probably be sent. He owes the village headman 3 Goerliz Marks, shall provide pledge for it, to pay it the next day.

Early Modern Bohemian German:
Hans Nicht Von Mildenaw, demnach er mit seiner Brudern Christoffen in grosse Vneinigkait gerathen, vnd in etwas bezüchtigt welcher er nit erweisen können, Ist der wegen gefenglich eingezogen vnd zue Pürgen Außgeben, dergestalt, das fort hin mit seinem brudern friedlich leben, Vnd sein guett Zwischen dato vnd Bartholomej Verkauffen sol, bey d. Translation to English:
Hans Nicht von Mildenau, since he got into great disunity with his brother, and accused him of something which he could not prove, for this reason he is taken into imprisonment and released on pledges, as follows, that from now on he shall live with his brother peacefully and shall sell his farm between now and St Bartholomew's, on pain of 20 Schocks, for which are pledges Hans Limbs the Younger from Mildenau, Georg Neuman from Mildenau, signed 20 May 1590


Example 5
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Richter Sagett Vnd clagett, dz Joachim Jacobiz ein rede Von Ihme ausbracht hette, dz er dz redt damitt Christof Jeschke weg. seiner Vbelthat gerädert selbst in die Stadt Friedtland bracht Vnd getragen, bier Zapfen, Polzen draus machen Vnd allerley Zauberey damit gebraucht, dem wer aber nicht also, bittett Jacobiz. beweiß aufzuerlegen, wie er dan wofern Jacobiz solches erweißen würde, wie es nimmermehr geschehen könte, er alles leib vnd leben Vnd was Ihme gott Vnter dieser herrschafft gescheret, Verlustig sein wolte, bittett Vmb Ambtes schuz Vnd hulffe auch rechtermeßigen beweiß.

Translation to English:
The magistrate complains that Joachim Jacobiz uttered speech, that he brought the wheel with which Christof Jeschke was wheeled on account of his evil deed itself into the town of Friedland, and made beer taps and furs out of it and thereby made use of all sorts of magic, however this is not true, he asks Jacobiz to provide evidence, and then insofar as Jacobiz is not able to demonstrate this, as he will never be able to do, he will lose everything of body and life and what God has granted him under this manor, asks for protection and help from the manorial court and also just evidence.

Secretarius, besonderß gutter gönner Vndt freundt, Nebens wünschung von gott dem allmechtig. aller sehlig. wolfarth vndt erbittung meiner freundlich. dienst, Soll dem selben ich nicht Perg., wied. am Negst. Junckehr daheimen were, welchs ihm die Zimmerleute zur andtwort geb., d. Kirchschreiber Zur Nachrichtung abgeschrieb. haben. da ich den schreiber hierumb gefraget, mich weg. des patents zu bericht., giebet mir d. schreyber zur anttwort, der botte sey ihm wie obgemelt, bey d. Vmbtragend. patents, Vnbeschweret schriefftlich. berichts mit zeig.n zu vberschick., hiernach Ich sampt Meinen Vnterthanen gehorsambst Inhalts Zu erseh., damit mir solchs nicht zu gemeßen, als hette ichs vorsezlich. weise hindan gesezt, dat: Vllerstorff den 22 May Ao p 1618. p.

Translation to English:
Honourable and well-renowned lord secretary, especially good patron and friend, in addition to wishing from God Almighty all blessed well-being, and pleading my friendly service, I should not hide from the latter, how last Saturday, just as I was on the field not far behind my courtyard, the messenger from my gracious lord his grace's manorial court of Friedlandt at that time came into my courtyard and stepped up to my chamber-people, and spoke to them and said, he was bringing a patent from the manorial court of Friedlandt and asked the latter further, whether I or the squire were at home, to which the chamber-person gave him in answer, the squire had just gone out the gate behind the courtyard, but the messenger did not at all want to wait, but rather said, the church-clerk had met him beside the Zittaw. Neißbreck, he would probably report it to the squire what kind of a patent I was bringing, which I learned from the chamber-people, as if the church-clerk was supposed to have copied it down for information. When I asked the clerk concerning this, to report to me concerning the patent, the clerk gave me in answer, the messenger had encountered him, as reported, beside the Zittisch Neiß Brecken, but because the poor old clerk had not had his spectacles with him, therefore without them had had not been able to read through the patent, and had spoken further to the messenger, he should indeed go to the squire in the court and show the patent, and also when my boy went to the messenger in my tavern in Vllerstorff and said to him, my squire is now at home, bring him round the patent, the messenger thereupon said, he did not have the time, he had to depart, and however excused himself, as reported, with the clerk. Now since the messenger was so very oppositional in running away without having completed his task, I ask in a friendly way, that the latter not be left unspoken-to about this, and that I be sent an unburdened written report of this patent which is being carried around, according to which I and my serfs can orient ourselves obediently to its contents, so that it will not be thought of me that I ignored it in a oppositional way, dated Vllerstorff 22 May 1618.

Example 9
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Heute dato den 17 Decemb. Ao. 1609 ist Jacob Weiße Zu Priedlanz neben seinem Vater Christof Weißen auch itzigen Scholzen hansen hartig daselbsten im Ambt alhier erschienen, Ambt gehorsamblichen berichtendt, demnach er vor Zwey Jahren Christof Leubners seligen Tochter daselbsten eine Waise geheiratet, welche ihme fast in die fünfHundert sß. Zubracht, Als hette sein lieber vater ihme hierkegen mit Zwey hundert sß. zuhelffen vorwilliget, so er auch nunmehr volstendig entpfangen, vnd zu erkauffung seines itz inhabenden guts angewendet, thuen solches entpfangs doch vnbeschadet seines Künfftigen gebührenden Erbfahls hiermit Ledig und loß sagende, derowegen obig gedachter Christof Waise, als Jacob Weisen Vater gebeten, dieses im Ambt zu Registriren vnd ihme deßen schrifftlichen schein weil hierümb der Schulthes zu Priedlanz gutte wissenschafft mitzutheilen, welches ich ihme also nicht vorwiedern Sondern ihme diese vnter meiner handtschrifft schein wieder fahren laßen Actum aufm Schlos Friedland den.17. Decemb: Ao. 1609.

Translation to English:
On today's date 17 Dec 1609 Jacob Weisse in Priedlanz, along with his father Christof Weissen, also the current village headman Hans Hartig there, appeared in the manorial court here, obediently reporting to the manorial court, that since 2 years ago he married the daughter of the dead Christof Leubner there, who brought him almost 500 Schocks, therefore his beloved father agreed to help him with 200 Schocks in return, which he has now fully received, and used for the purchasing of the farm he is currently occupying, and hereby declares free and released of what he has received, although without harming his future proper inheritance, for which reason the above-mentioned Christof Waise, as Jacob Weis's father, asks that this be registered in the manorial court and that he be issued with a written certificate concerning it, the village headman of Priedlanz can impart good knowledge of this, which could not therefore be refused him but rather this certificate was issued to him under my hand, Actum Friedland castle 17 Dec 1609.

Translation to English:

Example 14
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Demnach er hans Vlman daselbest, Von 8 Jahren hero 50 sß furgeliehen geldt, Schuldig Vorblieben Vnd hanß Vlman nit Allein bezahlt sondern Auch Weiln die Zahlung An Jtzo Ohne Gregor Neümans Schaden nit geschehen konnen, Er derselben Auff künfftig gewieß Vnd Zue sichtert Zue sein begehret, Als haben sie sich bayde heutte Acto Vorm Ampt berechnet das Gregor Neüman dem hanß Vlman Auf heutt mit haupt summa Vnd Zinsen baar Schuldig 66 sß, die er seinem gläubiger Auffs negst künfftige Jahr dingen deß 1606 Jahres sampt diß Jahres Zinsen Wo er Ihme die Auß guettem Willen mit nach lassen wolte, Ab zue legen vnd einzustellen, schuldig sein soll Vnd Will hierüber hat er Zue Versicherung Pürgen gesetzet hanß Richter Schulteß Zu Schönewalda, Actum den 24 May Ao 1605.

Translation to English:

Example 15
Early Modern Bohemian German:
Christoff Ansorge, so 13 ihar ein Melter gewesen saget aus, Wan die Fuhrleute pflegten abzuladen, Vnd denselbe abgemeßen wurde, hielten sie dießer gebrauch, wan ahn lezten schll ettwan ein Meßel oder Zwej Vbrig, pflegten sie daßelbe dem Maltzhern zuuorehren, welche nachmals daßelbe zusammen schnitten, vnd so sie ein viertel, oder zwej gesamblet daßelbe entweder Vntter sich zuteilen oder auff den hauffen zuschnitten Vnd ahn ein geldt zuschlagen, dießes hette ehr vielmals also gesehen.

Translation to English:
Christoff Ansorge, who has been a malter for 13 years, declares: when the carters use to unload wheat and it was measured out, they had this custom, that when on the last bushels a small measure or two was left over, they used to give it as an honorarium to the malt-lords, who afterwards cut it together, and when they had collected a quarters or two, either divided it among themselves or cut it in the heap and assessed it in money; this he had often observed.

Septembris Ao p 88.

Translation to English:
Since Mathess Gutbier, smith in Heinersdorf, defamed Michel Schwerdtner as a villain and a fraud in the village court in Heinersdorf in front of the jurymen's bench, and also laid hands on Schwerdtnern and beat him bloody, for which reason he was taken into imprisonment and today 27 Sep released from prison as follows, that he shall live peacefully with Michel Schwertner on paths and stairs, for which reason he named Paul Muehlessen in Heinersdorf as a pledge, that if he attacks Schwertner in the least the pledge will pay 10 Schocks fine without any delay, likewise Michel Schwernter has to live peacefully, named Gregor Berger as a pledge on pain of the above fine, signed 27 Sep 1588
